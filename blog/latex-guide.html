Why I Stopped Using Word for Engineering Reports

We've all been there. You spend 2 hours formatting an image in Microsoft Word, you move it 1mm to the left, and suddenly your entire 50-page thesis explodes. The font changes, the footer disappears, and chaos ensues.

Precision Matters

As engineers, we value precision. LaTeX is not a word processor; it is a document preparation system. It separates content from formatting. This means I can focus on the technical analysis, and let LaTeX handle the layout.

Handling Equations

The main reason I switched to LaTeX during my B.Sc. at Amirkabir University was the equations. In marine engineering, we deal with complex integrals and matrices.

Writing Navier-Stokes equations in Word is a nightmare. In LaTeX, it's beautiful logic:

\begin{equation}
  \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\rho \mathbf{u}) = 0
\end{equation}

My Templates

Over the last year, I've developed over 15 templates for technical documentation, ranging from lab reports to full ship design specifications. This ensures that every document leaving my desk maintains a high standard of professional typography.
