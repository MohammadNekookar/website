Hull Stability Challenges in Small Autonomous Vehicles

Designing a massive cargo ship is hard. Designing a small, 1-meter autonomous drone to survive coastal waves is a different kind of hard. When scaling down, physics doesn't always play nice.

The Scale Effect

In my recent project, prototyping a 1:20 scale ASV (Autonomous Surface Vehicle), the primary challenge was the Center of Gravity (CG). In full-scale ships, machinery is low. In small drones, sensors, cameras, and antennas are mounted high, raising the CG and threatening stability.

Design Choices: Monohull vs Catamaran

We initially considered a monohull design for speed. However, initial simulations in Maxsurf showed poor roll stability for the sensor payload. We switched to a Catamaran configuration.

  • Pros: High initial stability (GM), large deck area for solar panels.
  • Cons: Snap roll behavior in choppy water.

Simulation & Validation

Using Ansys AQWA, we simulated wave interactions. The results forced us to redesign the battery compartments, moving them into the twin hulls (sponsons) to act as ballast.

The final prototype achieved self-righting capabilities in waves up to 0.5m, a significant milestone for a vessel of this size.
