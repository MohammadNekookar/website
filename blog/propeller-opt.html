Optimizing Propeller Efficiency with CFD

Fuel efficiency is the number one priority for modern shipping. While hull form optimization gets a lot of attention, the propeller is where the power meets the water. A 1% increase in propeller efficiency can save thousands of dollars in fuel annually.

The Limit of Standard Series

Traditionally, we used the B-Series charts to select a propeller. While effective, these charts are based on open-water tests from decades ago. They don't account for the complex wake field behind a specific ship hull.

Using Ansys CFX

By using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), we can simulate the propeller rotating in the actual wake of the ship. This allows us to see cavitation pockets and pressure pulses that standard charts miss.

In a recent study, I analyzed a Kappel propeller design which uses curved tips to reduce induced drag (similar to winglets on an airplane). The simulation showed a 3.5% efficiency gain over a standard conventional propeller.

Conclusion

Coupling Python scripts to automate the geometry generation in Ansys allows us to iterate through hundreds of blade shapes overnight, finding the global optimum rather than just a local one.
